This project examines pediatric asthma hospitalizations across Texas counties during 2019, prior to the COVID-19 pandemic.

The Texas Department of State Health Services (DSHS) provides public information on chronic diseases through the Texas Health Data Portal, including county-level asthma hospitalization rates. However, the dashboard does not provide age-specific hospitalization data and does not allow access to the underlying patient-level records needed for pediatric asthma analyses.

Source: https://healthdata.dshs.texas.gov/dashboard/diseases/chronic-disease

To address this limitation, I used the Texas Inpatient Public Use Data File (PUDF), a de-identified hospital discharge dataset maintained by DSHS. This dataset includes patient age, diagnosis codes, discharge year, and county information, enabling age-specific and geographic analyses that are not available through the public dashboard.

Source: https://www.dshs.texas.gov/center-health-statistics/texas-health-care-information-collection/download-and-purchase-data/texas-inpatient-public-use-data-file-pudf/public-use-data-file-pudf-inpatient-free-download

Using the inpatient PUDF, I identified pediatric hospitalizations (patients under 18 years of age) with a primary diagnosis of asthma (ICD-10 codes J45–J46). Hospitalization records were aggregated by county and quarter to examine geographic variation and seasonal patterns in pediatric asthma hospitalizations across Texas.

Although the current analysis focuses on 2019 data, the same methodology can be extended to additional years of PUDF data to evaluate long-term trends in pediatric asthma hospitalizations before and after the COVID-19 pandemic.
